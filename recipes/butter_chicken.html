Butter Chicken

A goat and great introduction to Indian cuisine

This is a decadent curry-esque popular in countires like the United Kingdom.

This is only the surface of Indian food but is great nonetheless

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 tablespoons ground tumeric
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala
  • 1 1/2 sups full-fat Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 3 pounds chicken thighs
  • 1/4 pound unsalted butter
  • 4 teaspoons neutral oil
  • 2 yellow onions
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 3 tablespoons fresh ginger, grated and peeled
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 red chiles
  • Salt to taste
  • 2/3 cup chicken stock
  • 1 1/2 cup of cream
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons tomato paste
  • 3 tabloespoons ground almonds
  • cilantro to garnish

Recipe Instructions

  1. Whisk the yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, and cumin in a bowl. Place the chicken in the bowl and coat in the mixture. Cover and refrigerate for around a day.
  2. In a large pan on medium heat, melt the butter in the oil until it foams. Add the onions and cook until transculcent. Add the garlic, ginger, and cumin seeds. Cook until the onions caramelize.
  3. Add the cinnamon stick, tomatoes, chiles, and salt. Cook until the chiles are soft.
  4. Add the chicken into the pan and cook for around 5 minutes and then add the chicken stock. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer, uncovered, for approximately 30 minutes.
  5. Stir in the cream and tomato paste, and simmer until the chicken is cooked through, about 10 to 15 minutes.
  6. Add the almonds and cook for an additional 5 minutes removed from the heat. Garnish with the cilantro leaves.
